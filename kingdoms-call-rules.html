1. Overview & Objective

Kingdoms Call is a play-by-email (PBM) fantasy strategy game for 2–8 players and one Game Master (GM). Players rule rival kingdoms on a shared hex map, directing Kings and Heroes to explore provinces, recruit armies, fight battles, clear monster-infested features for treasure, hire wandering heroes, and eventually dominate the world.

The game runs for a set number of turns (typically 8–16). At the end, the player with the highest combined score — measured in province tax value, merit points, gold, and magic items — is crowned World Emperor. Seven other Imperial Offices are also awarded.

The GM's role: The GM creates the game, runs the map generator, resolves each turn, and distributes turn reports to players. The GM also arbitrates any disputes and may enter orders on behalf of players who are unavailable.

2. Setting Up a Game

2.1 Creating a Game (GM)

The GM logs into the GM Portal and creates a new game, setting:

  • Game name
  • Maximum players (2–8)
  • Map size: Small (8×6, up to 4 players), Medium (12×8, up to 6), Large (16×10, up to 8)
  • Game length: Short (8 turns), Standard (12 turns), Long (16 turns)

Once the game is created it appears as "Open" in the Player Portal for registered players to join.

2.2 Joining a Game (Player)

Players log into the Player Portal and click an open game. Each player sets:

  • King's Name — this is also your kingdom name
  • Race: Human, Elven, Dwarven, or Orcish
  • Alignment: Divine, Good, Druidic, Neutral, Pagan, Evil, or Undead
  • Temper: Berserk, Brave, Cautious, or Cowardly
  • 4 starting skill points distributed across any abilities, maximum 2 per skill

2.3 Starting Conditions

When the GM runs Turn 0, each player receives:

  • A home province (tax 10, 100% explored, race matches your king's race, army types match your race)
  • A King with base skills Melee 2, Archery 1, Sage 1, plus your 4 chosen points. Divine kings gain +1 White Magic; Undead kings gain +1 Necromancy. HP: 22.
  • 2 starting Heroes, each with 3 random starting skill levels
  • 1 starting army unit (Average quality, race-appropriate type for your home terrain)
  • 10 gold in the treasury

Home provinces are always assigned with at least 2 land neighbours, are spread as far apart as possible, and are never surrounded entirely by sea.

3. Turn Structure

3.1 The Five Phases

Each game turn is divided into five phases, resolved in order:

PhaseNameNotes
1SpringFirst actions of the turn
2Early Summer
3Late Summer
4FallTax is collected at end of this phase
5Early WinterArmy and hero wages are paid at end of this phase

Every character must have one order assigned for each of the 5 phases before a turn can be resolved. Orders are submitted by players via the Player Portal before the GM runs the turn.

3.2 Initiative Order

Within each phase, characters act in descending order of their total ability score (sum of all 15 skill levels). The March ability counts triple for initiative purposes. Characters with Defend orders always act last.

3.3 The Turn Cycle

  1. GM runs the turn — all 5 phases resolve in sequence
  2. GM distributes new turn reports to each player
  3. Players review their turn reports and submit orders for the next turn
  4. When all players have submitted, the GM runs the next turn

4. Characters — Kings & Heroes

4.1 Kings

Every player controls exactly one King. The King cannot be hired away and does not have a wage cost. Kings start with:

  • Base skills: Melee 2, Archery 1, Sage 1
  • +4 player-chosen points (max 2 per skill at setup)
  • Alignment bonus: Divine +1 White Magic, Undead +1 Necromancy
  • 22 HP
  • 1 starting army (Average quality)

4.2 Heroes

Heroes are independent characters that serve your kingdom for a wage. Each hero starts with exactly 3 random skill levels distributed across any of the 15 abilities. Their wage is 1 gold per turn for every 4 total skill levels (rounded up, minimum 1g).

When heroes gain skills through practice, their wage increases accordingly. If you cannot pay a hero's wage at the end of Early Winter, there is a chance they will desert and become wandering heroes again.

4.3 Wandering Heroes

Every non-home land province starts the game with one wandering hero, generated exactly like a starting hero (3 random skill levels, random race/alignment/temper). At the very end of each turn, each wandering hero has a 35% chance to drift to a random adjacent land province.

You can see wandering heroes in provinces you own or have previously visited. They can be hired using the Hire Hero order.

4.4 Hiring Heroes

A character in the same province as a wandering hero can issue a Hire Hero order. If you can afford the wage, the hero joins immediately. A newly hired hero may receive orders starting from the phase after they were hired, not the current phase.

4.5 Alignment & Temper

Alignment affects fanaticism (see Battle System) and available spells. The seven alignments are: Divine, Good, Druidic, Neutral, Pagan, Evil, Undead.

Temper affects personal combat behaviour:

TemperEffect in Encounters/Duels
BerserkAlways challenges to duel. +10% to hit. +1–4 bonus HP from rage. May fight recklessly.
BraveAlways challenges to duel. +10% to hit (Elan bonus).
CautiousMay decline duels (25% chance). Opponents –10% to hit (Parrying). May attempt to flee when at low HP.
CowardlyOften flees duels (75% chance). A fleeing commander demoralizes own troops –25%.

4.6 Character HP

Characters have Hit Points (HP) representing their personal health. HP lost in personal combat or encounters persists between turns. Characters can recover HP by resting (2–5 HP per Rest order). If a character reaches 0 HP they are slain.

5.1 Practising Skills

A character can spend a phase using the Practice Skill order. Base success chance: 35%. Group training bonuses apply:

  • Training alongside a character with a higher level in the same skill: automatic success
  • Training alongside one or more characters at the same level: success chance doubled (70%)
  • These bonuses apply across players — a wandering hero or enemy character in the same province with the same training order also counts

6. Movement

6.1 Move Points

Each character has a pool of Move Points for the entire turn (not per phase). One Move order uses one Move point to enter an adjacent province. Conquering a province costs an additional Move point on top of the entry cost.

Character typeMove points per turn
Hero with no armies5
Hero with armiesEqual to the slowest army's Speed rating

If a character runs out of Move points, their Move orders in later phases are ignored. A hero who garrisons all their armies mid-turn immediately regains 5 Move points for the rest of the turn.

Example: A hero carrying one Human Garrison (Speed 1) and one Infantry (Speed 3) has Move points = 1 (the slowest). They move North East in Spring (1 MP used, 0 left). They cannot move again this turn. If they garrison the Garrison unit in that province, they now have Speed = 3 (Infantry only) — but their MP pool does not reset; MPs only reset when all armies are left behind.

6.2 Move Orders

Move orders specify a compass direction, not a destination province. Directions are: North West (NW), North East (NE), West (W), East (E), South West (SW), South East (SE). The player portal shows what terrain is in each direction, whether a province is owned, and how many defenders it has.

6.3 Sea Movement

Army compositionCan enter sea provinces?
No armies (hero alone)Yes — freely
Only flying armies (Eagle Riders)Yes — freely
Only sea armies (Fleets etc)Yes, but cannot move directly land→land

A hero with only sea armies may not move directly from one land province to another unless both provinces are adjacent to the same sea province.

6.4 Map Wraparound

The world map wraps east-to-west like a cylinder. Moving off the eastern edge brings a character to the western edge of the same row, and vice versa. The map does not wrap north-to-south — the top and bottom edges are hard boundaries.

7. The World Map

The map is a hex grid of pointy-top hexagons in one of three sizes. Each hex is a province.

7.1 Map Sizes

8. Provinces & Terrain

8.1 Province Properties

  • Tax value — gold collected per turn during Fall (1–10; home province always starts at 10)
  • Race — determines which army types are recruitable
  • Terrain — affects battle line width, army bonuses, and exploration speed
  • Exploration % — 0% to 100%; features are only discovered through exploration
  • Features — 1–5 per land province (average ~3), each guarding treasure and a magic item

8.2 Terrain Types

TerrainExploration/turnBattle line widthNotes
Plain—5–10Cavalry and Knights get bonus here
Forest—3–6Elven units get terrain bonus
Mountain—1–3Dwarven units get terrain bonus
Desert—5–10Reduces some cavalry penalties
Jungle—3–6Orcish and Elven skirmishers get bonus
SeaN/A5–10Only naval armies and heroes alone may enter

8.3 Exploration

A province's exploration percentage starts at 0% for unclaimed provinces (and 100% for your home province). Each Explore order increases it by 10% × Explorer skill level, minimum 10% per action. As exploration increases, undiscovered features may be revealed. A feature can only be searched once it is discovered.

8.4 Neutral Defenders

Every non-home land province starts with 1–3 neutral army units guarding it, drawn randomly from the province's locally available army types, always at Average quality. These defenders must be defeated to claim the province. Defenders who repel an attack can gain experience. Province info (visible to characters in adjacent provinces or via Spy) shows what unit types are defending and their total strength.

8.5 Claiming Provinces

A character with at least one army can claim an undefended neutral province by moving into it (costs 1 Move point for entry). Defeating neutral defenders or an enemy's forces to take a province costs 1 additional Move point (the conquest cost). Claiming a province earns merit points equal to the province's tax value.

9. Economy — Gold & Wages

9.1 Tax Collection

At the end of Fall (phase 4), each player collects gold equal to the sum of tax values of all provinces they own. Heroes with the Ring of Wealth item add +2g to this collection.

9.2 Army Wages

At the end of Early Winter (phase 5), each player pays wages equal to the recruitment cost of every army they own (carried armies + garrison armies in owned provinces):

9.3 Hero Wages

Heroes cost 1g per turn for every 4 total skill levels (minimum 1g, rounded up). The King has no wage cost. If you cannot pay a hero, they may desert (40% chance per hero) and become a wandering hero again. Kings never desert.

9.4 Starting Gold

Every kingdom begins with 10 gold.

10. Armies

10.1 Army Experience Tiers

Every army unit has an experience tier affecting its combat power:

TierQuality multiplierGained from

After a lost battle, armies have a 40% chance to be demoted one tier. The Oratory spell instantly promotes all of a caster's armies by one tier.

10.2 Recruiting Armies

Armies are recruited using the Recruit Armies order. Restrictions:

  • You must own the province to recruit normal armies
  • In a province you've previously visited but don't own, you may still recruit locally available army types, but only 1 per type
  • Generic Garrison units can be recruited anywhere regardless of ownership or prior knowledge, for 1g each
  • Limits per game turn (across all phases combined):
    • Normal provinces: 1 of each army type per province per turn
    • Home province (tax 10): 2 of each type per turn
    • All Garrison types: 2 per game turn from any province

Multiple different army types may be recruited in a single Recruit order. Newly recruited armies start at Average experience.

10.4 Combat Strength Formula

Each unit's combat value in a specific battle = Base Strength × Quality Multiplier × Terrain Modifier × Type-Matchup Modifier

  • Terrain modifier: ×1.25 on bonus terrain, ×0.75 on penalty terrain, ×1.0 otherwise
  • Type-matchup modifier: ×1.2 if opposing force contains a category this unit is Strong vs; ×0.8 if it contains one this unit is Weak vs

The Total Army Strength shown in turn reports and kingdom panels is a simplified estimate: Base Strength × Quality Multiplier only, without situational modifiers.

11. Battle System

11.1 When Battle Occurs

Battle occurs when a character leading armies moves into a province owned or defended by an enemy. Battles are resolved instantly during turn resolution.

11.2 The Duel

Before the main battle, the two supreme commanders may fight a personal duel:

  • Berserk or Brave commanders always challenge
  • Cautious commanders may decline (25% chance)
  • Cowardly commanders usually flee (75% chance) — this demoralizes their own troops by –25% strength

The duel proceeds in rounds: first an archery phase (free shots equal to Archery skill level), then melee rounds. Base hit chance: 50%, modified ±10% per level of Melee advantage. Berserk adds rage HP (1–4 extra). Brave adds +10% to hit (Elan). Cautious reduces opponent's hit chance by 10% (Parrying). The losing commander's army is demoralized –25% for the battle.

11.3 Battle Lines

Armies are sorted best-first and placed into battle lines. Line width is determined by terrain:

  • Mountain: 1–3 wide
  • Forest / Jungle: 3–6 wide
  • Plain / Desert / Sea: 5–10 wide

Front-line armies fight at 100% strength. Second-line armies at 50%. Third line and beyond at 25%. Wider terrain therefore advantages larger forces more significantly.

11.4 Tactical Bonus

The supreme commander (character with most armies; Tactical skill as tiebreaker) applies their Tactical skill: each level multiplies the entire force's strength by ×1.2. A character with Tactical 3 leads armies at ×1.6 effective strength.

11.5 Defender Advantages

  • Defenders always fight at ×1.25 strength
  • Defenders in a home province (tax 10) add a further ×1.2 bonus
  • Attackers who have previously explored the province gain up to +25% bonus from terrain knowledge

11.6 Fanaticism

At the start of each battle, armies may fight at the next quality tier (e.g. Average fights as Veteran) depending on their kingdom's alignment:

AlignmentFanaticism chance
Divine50%
Good40%
Evil25%
Pagan15%
Druidic20%
Neutral10%
Undead0% (but Skeletal Legions are always fanatical)

11.7 Pursuit

When a battle is won, fast units pursue the retreating enemy. Cavalry and Heavy units have a 75% chance to pursue; Infantry-category units have a 25% chance. Retreating armies fight at +25% defence but –1 quality tier. Outrun units may be destroyed.

11.8 Aftermath

  • Winning armies: 35% chance to promote one experience tier
  • Losing armies: 40% chance to demote one experience tier
  • Depleted and Green units that lose may scatter into the local population
  • Defenders who repel an attack also gain the 35% promotion chance

11.9 Conquest

If the attacker wins, they claim the province. Defender's garrison armies are eliminated. Attacker earns merit points equal to the province's tax value.

12. Personal Encounters & Features

12.1 Features

Each land province has 1–5 features (dungeons, lairs, ruins, ancient battlefields, etc). Features are hidden until the province is explored. Clearing a feature yields gold (based on the monster's difficulty) and one random magic item.

12.2 Searching Features

The Search Feature order attempts to clear a discovered feature. The encounter is resolved using the character's Encounter Plan.

12.3 Encounter Plans

Each character has a persistent Encounter Plan: an ordered list of up to 6 tactics. The plan is executed in order until the encounter ends. If you run out of tactics, Melee is used by default. You set and update encounter plans in the orders form each turn.

TacticCodeEffect
MeleeMEClose to hand-to-hand combat. Uses Melee skill. Brave/Berserk temper adds +10% to hit.
Fire ArrowFIShoot arrows before closing. Archery skill level = number of free shots (30%+10%/level hit chance). Requires Archery ≥ 1.
Cast SpellCAUse a combat spell. Requires a magic school ≥ 1. 30%+15%/level cast chance; 4–16 damage on success.
TalkTAAttempt to parley. 20%+15%/level Druid skill chance of success. On success, creature steps aside.

12.4 Encounter Narrative

Each Search Feature action produces a detailed narrative in your turn report: the approach to the feature, a description of the guardian, then step-by-step combat or negotiation results. Win or lose, you receive a full account of what happened.

12.5 Army Strength in Encounters

Each army unit you carry adds to your combat power in feature encounters, though without the full battle-line system (armies add their base strength directly).

16. Intelligence & Fog of War

16.1 What You Can See

Your turn report contains only information your kingdom has earned:

  • Your own characters' actions — full detail on every order, success or failure, and why
  • Public events in provinces you own or have characters in — battles, conquests, seizures visible to you if they happen in your territory
  • [Intelligence] notifications — flagged entries when enemy heroes enter your owned provinces, or when heroes from other kingdoms are in the same province as one of your characters. These describe the enemy force in terms your best Spy skill can determine.
  • Wandering hero sightings — when a wandering hero enters one of your owned provinces, you are notified

16.2 What You Cannot See

  • Other players' routine actions in provinces you don't own or visit
  • What orders other players are planning
  • The exact composition of enemy armies (unless your Spy skill is level 4–5)
  • The total number of undiscovered features in your provinces — only your exploration % and known features are shown

16.3 Army Intelligence Quality

When an enemy force is reported via [Intelligence] notifications, accuracy depends on your best Spy skill across all your characters:

Best Spy levelWhat you see
0–1A vague impression: "a moderate force" / "a powerful force"
2–3Troop categories and rough counts: "3 cavalry-type, 2 infantry-type (5 total)"
4–5Exact unit types and numbers: "2× Knights, 1× Longbowmen, 1× Infantry"

17. Victory Conditions

17.1 End of Game

The game ends after the set number of turns. Final scores are calculated and Imperial Offices awarded.

17.2 Final Score

Each kingdom's total score = (Tax base × 4) + (Total merits × 2) + (Gold ÷ 3) + (Magic items × 3)

  • Tax base = sum of tax values of all provinces owned at game end
  • Total merits = kingdom merits + sum of all character merits (earned from conquering, clearing features, personal combat)
  • Gold = treasury at game end
  • Magic items = total items held by all characters

17.3 Imperial Offices

Eight offices are awarded in score order (highest score gets first choice of office):

18. For the Game Master

18.1 GM Portal

The GM Portal is at kingdoms-call-gm-portal.html. From here you can:

  • Create new games and configure their settings
  • Monitor which players have submitted orders each turn
  • Enter orders on behalf of any player (useful if a player is unavailable)
  • Run Turn 0 to generate the map and push starting turn reports
  • Resolve turns (all 5 phases run automatically, turn reports pushed to players)
  • Delete games

18.2 Running Turn 0

Once enough players have joined (minimum 2), a "Run Turn 0" button appears. Clicking it:

  1. Generates the world map based on the configured size
  2. Places player starting provinces (spread apart, at least 2 land neighbours, race matching each king)
  3. Populates all provinces with neutral defenders, wandering heroes, and features
  4. Creates all starting characters with correct skills and starting armies
  5. Pushes individual turn reports to each player

18.3 Resolving Turns

When all players have submitted orders, click "Resolve Turn & Generate New Reports". The system:

  1. Merges all player orders (including encounter plans)
  2. Applies defaults (Defend) to any missing orders
  3. Runs all 5 phases in initiative order
  4. Collects tax at Fall, pays wages at Early Winter
  5. Moves wandering heroes (35% drift chance per hero)
  6. Generates per-player turn reports with appropriate fog of war
  7. Pushes reports to all players and resets submitted flags

18.4 Entering Orders for Players

In the game detail view, each player's character orders can be filled in by the GM. This is useful when a player hasn't submitted by the deadline. GM-entered orders override player-submitted orders.

18.5 Map Generation Rules

  • Province names are generated randomly from prefix+suffix combinations
  • Terrain is weighted so sea provinces appear more on edges
  • Starting provinces are guaranteed to have tax 10, 100% exploration, no defenders, and at least 2 land neighbours
  • Players are placed as far apart as possible
  • Province race always matches the king's race for home provinces
Kingdoms Call is inspired by the original Adventurer Kings by David Snell (Ark Royal Games, 1991–1997).
