📜 Kingdoms Call — Rulebook

Adapted from the original Kingdoms Call rules (David Snell / Ark Royal Games, 1991–1997) for solo/GM-run play.

Overview

Each player controls a King, two or more Heroes, armies, and a treasury. The game runs for several turns, each split into 5 phases: Spring, Early Summer, Late Summer, Fall, Early Winter. Before the GM resolves a turn, every character must have an order set for every one of the 5 phases.

Turn Order — Resolution Sequence

  1. Phase 1 (Spring) orders resolve for all characters, in initiative order.
  2. Phase 2 (Early Summer) orders resolve the same way.
  3. ...continues through Phase 5 (Early Winter).
  4. Within each phase: characters act in order of total ability (skills summed, March ×3). Characters ordered to Defend always act last. Move/Combat/Explore/etc. all resolve via this same initiative order — there's no separate "movement phase" vs "combat phase": each character's single action for that phase (which might be a Move that triggers combat) resolves in turn.
  5. At the end of Phase 4 (Fall): Taxes are collected from all owned provinces.
  6. At the end of Phase 5 (Early Winter): Army wages and hero wages are paid. Each army unit costs its recruitment price per turn to maintain (e.g. Infantry 2g, Cavalry 3g, Knights 4g). Heroes also cost wages based on their skill total. If a kingdom cannot pay, armies disband first (cheapest first — Skeletal Legions are free and never disband), then heroes desert.

The 15 Abilities — What Each One Actually Does

Every character has skill levels 0–9 in each ability below. Items can grant bonus levels on top of the base skill (shown in their tooltip / on the Items badge). "Effective level" used in calculations = base skill + any item bonuses.

At setup, each player distributes 4 free skill points across any abilities for their King (on top of the King's base Melee 2 / Archery 1 / Sage 1, plus an alignment bonus for Divine or Undead). Starting Heroes (both a kingdom's initial 2 heroes and every wandering hero found across the map) begin with exactly 3 randomly assigned skill levels.

A character can only cast spells from a school they have at least 1 level in. Spell success chance (for Action-type spells) = 15% + 15%×skill level, +15% if the caster holds an item with a magic-boosting effect, +10% if the caster's kingdom is Pagan-aligned.

Map Geometry

The world map wraps east-to-west like a cylinder: moving off the eastern edge brings a character to the western edge of the same row, and vice versa. The map does not wrap north-to-south — the northern and southern edges are hard boundaries. This wraparound is factored into all distance, movement, and pathing calculations (Move range, Pursue, neighbour lookups), so a character near the map's east or west edge can use the wrap as a shortcut to the other side.

Order Planning & Predicted Location

Because all 5 phases are planned before any of them resolve, the order editor predicts where each character will be standing for a given phase by replaying their already-queued Move orders for earlier phases of the same turn. If you set a Move for Spring and then open the Early Summer order for the same character, the Move dropdown (and any "who's here" lists, like Hire or Give Item) will be based on the destination of that Spring move — not their start-of-turn location. The order editor displays a small note showing exactly where the character is predicted to be for the phase you're editing.

Note: this prediction only tracks the acting character's own queued moves. It does not simulate combat outcomes, other characters' moves, or other unpredictable effects that might occur earlier in the turn — those are resolved live when you hit "Resolve Full Turn," and the Chronicle will narrate anything that changes the board from what was planned.

Every land province has 1–5 features (average 3): dungeons, lairs, ruins etc, each guarded by a monster. Defeating it yields 1–10g (avg ~3g) and a magic item. Every King's starting home province has its tax fixed at 10 (the richest possible value) and is never surrounded entirely by sea — it will always have at least 2 land neighbours.

Army Experience

Every army unit has an experience tier: Green → Average → Veteran → Elite → Crack. Each tier grants a combat strength multiplier (Green ×0.75, Average ×1.0, Veteran ×1.25, Elite ×1.5, Crack ×1.8). Newly recruited armies and NPC/neutral defenders always start at Average. After any battle, victorious armies have a chance to be promoted a tier (battle-hardening); defeated armies have a chance to be demoted a tier (attrition). The Oratory spell instantly promotes all of a caster's armies by one tier. Each army's current tier is shown next to it in the Kingdoms panel, colour-coded for quick reference.

Recruiting: in a province your kingdom owns, you may recruit any locally available army type, up to 2 of each type in the King's home province (tax 10) or 1 of each elsewhere. In a province you don't own but your kingdom has previously visited or owned ("Known to:" shown in the province panel), you may still recruit 1 of any locally available type — representing knowledge of what forces exist there, recruited at a distance — but never more than 1 per type since you lack local infrastructure. In a province with no prior knowledge and no ownership, only a generic Garrison (local levies) unit may be raised — always available anywhere, for 1g, representing rallying whatever militia is willing to fight regardless of who controls the land.

Wandering Heroes

Every land province that isn't a player's home province starts with one wandering hero, built exactly like a starting Hero (3 random skill levels, random race/alignment/temper). At the very end of each turn, each wandering hero has a 35% chance to drift to a random adjacent land province; otherwise they stay put. The province info panel shows which kingdoms are currently aware of a province ("Known to:") based on having owned it or had a character physically enter it — useful for the GM to judge what intel to share with which player.

Armies & the Sea

Each race/terrain combination has its own recruitable army types (e.g. Elven Bowmen in forests, Dwarven Guard in mountains). Sea provinces require Fleets — naval army units with Transport capacity — to carry land armies across. A character without a fleet cannot move into sea provinces while leading land armies.

Every neutral (unowned) land province starts guarded by 1–3 neutral defender armies, randomly drawn from that province's available unit types, always at Average experience.

Combat Strength & Unit Abilities

Each unit's effective combat strength = base Strength × Quality multiplier × Terrain modifier × Type-matchup modifier. Quality multipliers: Green ×1, Average ×2, Veteran ×3, Crack ×4, Elite ×5. Terrain: ×1.25 on bonus terrain, ×0.75 on penalty terrain. Type matchups: ×1.2 if the opposing force contains a category this unit counters, ×0.8 if it contains a category this unit is weak against.

A character's Total Army Strength (shown in the Kingdoms panel) is a quick baseline estimate: base strength × quality multiplier, without situational modifiers.

Battle System (Rules 7.0–7.8)

When a character leading armies moves into a province defended by enemies, a full battle is resolved automatically during turn resolution. Battles involve the following stages in order:

7.1 — Supreme Commanders. The character with the most armies on each side is the Supreme Commander. If tied, the one with the highest Tactical skill commands. The Supreme Commander's Tactical skill multiplies their entire force's strength by +20% per level.

7.2 — Battle Lines. Armies are sorted best-first and placed into a battle line whose length depends on terrain. Front-line armies fight at full strength. Armies in the second rank fight at 50% strength. Third rank and beyond fight at 25% strength. The battle line is shorter in mountains and forests (1–3 wide), and longer on plains and desert (5–10 wide). This means large forces get more value from their armies on open terrain and suffer more from the cramped conditions of mountains and jungle.

Defender Bonus. Defenders always fight at ×1.25 strength, representing the advantage of prepared positions. Home provinces add a further ×1.2 bonus. Attackers who have previously explored the province gain up to +25% from terrain knowledge.

Fanaticism. Each turn, armies may fight at the next quality level up depending on their kingdom's alignment. Divine kingdoms: 50% chance, Good: 40%, Druidic: 20%, Neutral: 10%, Pagan: 15%, Evil: 25%. Skeletal Legions are always fanatical for Undead kingdoms.

7.1 — The Duel. Before the main battle, the two Supreme Commanders may fight a personal duel. Berserk and Brave characters always challenge. Cautious characters may decline (25% chance). Cowardly characters usually flee (75% chance — and their fleeing demoralizes their own troops by 25%). The duel is resolved in rounds: first an archery exchange (free attacks equal to Archery skill), then melee rounds where each combatant hits on a base 50% chance, modified +10% per level of Melee advantage. Temper effects: Berserk — +1–4 extra HP from rage; Brave — +10% to hit (Elan); Cautious — opponents –10% to hit (Parrying); Cowardly — +10% flee chance. If a commander is slain, their force is demoralized (–25% strength) for the rest of the battle.

7.3 — Pursuit. When a battle is won, fast-moving units (cavalry, skirmishers) pursue the retreating enemy. Cavalry has a 75% chance to pursue, infantry 25%. In pursuit, retreating armies fight at +25% defense but –1 quality tier. Outrun units may be destroyed.

7.4 — Retreat. The losing force retreats to the province they came from. Depleted and Green-quality units scatter into the local population and are lost. Average and better units may survive the retreat.

7.5 — Aftermath. After the battle, winning armies have a 35% chance to be promoted one experience tier (battle-hardening). Losing armies have a 40% chance to be demoted one tier (attrition). Defenders who successfully repel an attack gain the same promotion chance as victors.

7.6 — Contested Provinces. If an attacker wins, they claim the province. If they lose, the province remains under its current ownership.

7.7 — Conquest. Conquering a province awards the attacker merit points equal to the province's tax value. The province's garrison armies are eliminated. The new owner can begin recruiting there next turn.

7.8 — Multiple Contingents. If multiple allied characters attack the same province in the same phase, their armies combine. The character with the most armies (or highest Tactical in a tie) becomes the Supreme Commander, and their Tactical bonus applies to the whole combined force.

Magic Items

Won by defeating a feature's monster. Each grants a passive bonus (skill +2, HP, gold, etc — see the item's tooltip for its exact effect) for as long as the holding character keeps it. Items can be freely given between characters sharing a province via the Give Magic Item order.

Victory

At game end, 8 Imperial Offices are awarded based on tax base, merits, and skill totals. World Emperor (largest tax base) is the highest prize.
